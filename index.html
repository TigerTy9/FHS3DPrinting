About Our Club

At the FHS3DPrinting Club, students can explore new technology, work on exciting projects, and learn valuable skills. We focus on both structured learning and creative freedom, allowing members to grow their expertise in 3D printing.

Club Structure & Meeting Schedule

Here's what you can expect at our weekly and monthly meetings:

  1. Kickoff Meeting: Introduction & Inspiration

    • Introduction to 3D printing with a showcase of exciting projects and applications.
    • Icebreaker activity: Design a simple 3D object, like a keychain, using Tinkercad.

  2. Weekly/Monthly Themes: Structured Learning

    • Skill-building workshops on 3D modeling software (Tinkercad, Blender, Fusion 360) and 3D printer troubleshooting.
    • Guest speakers and virtual tours to learn about real-world applications of 3D printing.

  3. Project-Based Learning: Collaborative & Fun

    • Work in teams on exciting projects, like designing board game pieces or phone holders.
    • Participate in friendly design challenges, like creating the most creative chess piece.
    • End-of-semester showcase where members present their projects to the school.

  4. Open Lab Sessions: Creative Freedom

    • Free design time to work on personal projects or collaborate with peers.
    • Peer mentoring sessions where experienced members help newcomers.

  5. Fun & Social Elements

    • Engage in 3D printing games, field trips, and themed design challenges.

  6. Final Project & Reflection

    • Work on a major group project, such as a 3D-printed mural or sculpture for the school.
    • Reflect on your learning and provide feedback to help improve the club for next year.
